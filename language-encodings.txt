ASCII is an single bytes, seven-bit encoding. There are other single byte, eight-bit encodings that extend ASCII.<check>

There are a set of encodings that extend ASCII that come under the general encoding called ISO 8859. This includes
8859-1 or Latin 1
8859-2 or Latin 2



 One of
the most popular is Latin-1, which also have the Internet Assigned Number of ISO/IEC 8859-1. It includes code points for most
western European languages, including the Norwegian, Swedish, Danish, German, Italian, and Spanish. It includes most of 
French with the exception of about two letters, for which there are workarounds.
